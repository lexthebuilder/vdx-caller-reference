Pre-Dial Checklist + Call Structure

Five things before you dial. Four phases on every call. No exceptions.

Before You Dial — Every Campaign, Every Time

1
Read the Quick Start Guide top to bottomICP first. Main problem second. Bar pitch third. It's a page and a half — no skipping.
2
Scan the company websiteKnow how they present to the outside world. Know how to pronounce their name.
3
Check the event name and datesKnow what you're inviting them to. Know the event name and the audience.
4
Open and test the booking linkMake sure it works and you know what the prospect will see before you're live on a call.
5
Say your bar pitch out loudIf you've never said it out loud, you're saying it for the first time on a real call. They'll hear it.
Credibility, not expertise.  You don't need to know everything about the customer. You need to sound like someone worth staying on the line with. Those are different things.

The Four Phases — Every Call, Every Time

01

Open

Confirm attendance. Create curiosity.

"Hey [Name] — this is [Alias] with [Company]. Quick question — are you heading to [Event]?"
YES →"You'll know in about 10 seconds if this is worth your time."
NO →Strong ICP fit? Pivot to virtual. Otherwise move on.
Confirm attendance first. Don't pitch until you know they're going.
02

Hook

Name their world before pitching yours.

"A lot of teams we connect with at these shows are dealing with [CORE PROBLEM] — is that something you're running into?"
ENGAGES →Engagement signal confirmed. Move to close.
UNSURE →Backup pain point or bar pitch. Apply ABP.
Lead with their world. Don't feature-dump before they've acknowledged relevance.
03

Close

Propose a time. Stay firm. No Gumby language.

"What day are you planning to walk the show floor?" → "Events get hectic — let me grab some time on your calendar. Does [TIME 1] or [TIME 2] work?"
Say this:
"I'll follow up before the meeting to make sure it still works."
"If your schedule shifts, we can always adjust."
Never this:
"No worries if you can't make it"  ·  "Feel free to cancel"  ·  "Totally low-key just drop by"
04

Confirm

Don't hang up until it's in their inbox.

"You should see that come through right now." → Stay on the line. Walk them through it if needed.
✓They confirm receipt — the meeting exists.
✗If it's not in their calendar, it does not exist.
Silence is fine. Do not fill it with more talking. Give them a moment.
Virtual? Do NOT use an event-specific link. Alert FDE immediately.

ABP Framework + Meeting Standards

Handle every objection. Know what counts. Know what doesn't.

ABP — Every Objection, Every Time

ACKNOWLEDGE

Validate what they said. Normalize it. Show empathy.
"That makes sense."
"Totally fair."
"You're not the first person to say that today."
"I get it — your time is valuable."

BRIDGE

Reframe toward the meeting. Shrink the ask.
"That's exactly why this conversation is worth having."
"Most teams say the same thing before they actually see it."
"It's 20 minutes — if it's not relevant, you'll know pretty quickly."

PIVOT

Get back to the ask. Always end with a question.
"What day are you planning to walk the show floor?"
"Since you're already going to be there, let's lock something in."
"Let me just find something while I have you."

When you don't know the answer

"Great question — that's exactly what this meeting is for. Our team would walk you through that. What day are you planning to walk the floor?"
Works for product, company, and proof questions. You don't need the answer. The meeting is the answer.

Common Objections

"Not familiar with what you guys do"
Fair — we help teams with [core problem]. Best way to see it is in person. What day are you walking the floor?
"We're already working with someone like this"
That makes sense. Not about replacing — worth seeing what we do differently. It's 20 minutes.
"Not sure this is relevant to us"
Fair point. Are you dealing with [core problem] at all? If it's even a little relevant, worth a quick chat.
"Too busy / bad time"
Totally get it. You're already going to be there — just about finding a window. What day works?
"I'm not the decision maker"
No problem. Is this relevant to what your team does day to day? That's really all I'm checking right now.

Meeting Booked Standard

Time confirmed
+
At least ONE engagement signal
Both required. One without the other is not a meeting booked.

Engagement Signals — Green Flags

References a specific pain or challenge unprompted
Brings up a competitor or existing solution
Asks a clarifying question about the product
Volunteers context about their role or team
Energy shifts — they slow down, they engage
Pulls up their calendar while still on the call

Red Flags — Not a Real Commitment

"Sure whatever works"
"Yeah go ahead and send something over"
Agreed before you explained what the meeting was about
Call under 60 seconds with no real back and forth
"Mm-hmm" with nothing to follow it

Never a Meeting — Do Not Book

Existing customer or partnerNot net new pipeline. Log as Connection Initiated. Pass context to FDE if worth sharing.
The Uno ReverseProspect is pitching you back. Disengage politely. Do not book it.
Third party proxy — "Jane is the right person"Jane isn't on the call. Jane hasn't agreed to anything. Log Connection Initiated and pass context to FDE.
The Gumby closeYou said "no worries if you can't make it." That meeting is already soft. Your language has to match the commitment.

Meeting Type

In-person 1:1

Prospect confirms they're attending the event. Default.

Registration

Customer-hosted event or activation. Follow script guidance.

Virtual

Strong ICP fit + NOT attending. Alert FDE immediately. Do NOT use an event-specific booking link. Wrong type = no show.

You are delivering on a customer's investment.  Book with that in mind.
